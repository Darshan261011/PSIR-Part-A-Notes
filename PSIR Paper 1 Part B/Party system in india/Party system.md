## Intro
- Edmund burke defines- political parties are voluntary associations of individuals who share the same social,political and economic ideology and who try to gain power to implement that ideology.

## Context
- party system does not mean no of political parties. It means no of pol parties having systemic relevance.- Sartori

## Importance of political parties
- These perform the task of interest aggregation and interest articulation – Gabriel and powell model  
- Laski held that democracy is inconceivalbe w/o pol parties.  
- Pol parties strenghthen the institutional basis of democracy. Too much “street politics” affects civic culture and has a danger of converting democratic political order into “grammar of anarchy”

## Phases of party system (Y Yadav)
- 1952-67 : congress dominance at the centre and at the states  
- 1967-89: congress at center regional Parties in states  
  - Calls it the first democratic upsurge due to rise of OBCs whom R&R call as “bullock capitalists.”  
  - Reasons for 1st Democratic upsurge: Green revolution and States reorg because of which regional parties dominated by OBCs( referred to as dominant castes) came to power.  
- 89-2014: coalition politics (era of mandal,masjid and market). Implementation of Mandal report, rise of dalit politics as sub altern entered politics, ex- BSP.  
- 2014-today: bjp dominance at the center and at the states  

### On decline of congress
- says Congress has become “pathetically irrelevant” and “it must die.”  
- It does not has a vision.  
- Obstacle to those who want to build an alternative political forum

## Features of Indian political parties (Zoya Hassan)
- No role of ideology. “Catch all” parties to increase voter base  
- No grand design of national development. Focus only on next forthcoming election.  
- There is a disconnect between people and parties and greater connection between political parties and corporate groups. Even socialist and communist parties links to corporate groups  
- Office oriented instead of policy oriented

- Rajni kothari-called Indian party system as Congress system.  
  - called Congress party of consensus and others party of pressure.  
  - It was a rainbow coalition of all existing ideologies.  
  - Distinguished because of its democratic culture.

## Decline of congress system

### External factors
- Y yadav: 1st democratic upsurge( rise of regional parties during 1960s) and 2nd democratic upsurge( rise of OBCs and SCs during 1990s)  
- Rise of regional parties due to Green Revolution and states reorganisation  
- Congress perceived as party of minorities hence lost the caste Hindu votes.  
- Emergence of Civil society groups that carried forward political agenda during the Emergency period.  

### Internal factors
- Lack of internal democracy  
- concentration of power in the hands of single leader  
- Lost touch with grassroots leaders  
- Top leadership was dependent on loyalist, no scope of internal criticism and dissent  
- Leadership crisis and internal factionalism—> rise of G23 within congress that wants radical internal changes  

---

## Rise of BJP system
- Suhas palshikar calls it fourth party system.  
  - Not just a BJP victory but a structural shift in INdian politics  
  - says BJP has become the central pole around which politics of India revolves.  
  - BJP’s twin agenda of Hindu nationalism and New developmentalism has captured the realm of ideas  

- Milan V of Carnegie endowment calls it india’s shift from multipolarity(coalition era) to unipolarity.  
- Many scholars have called the current phase as the second republic.  
- Uptil now indian politics was seen as centre left. Now it has clearly shifted towards the right.

## Rise of regional parties

### Reasons
#### Institutional
- delinking of parliament and state elections  
- India’s federal system under which state govts deal with day to day administration which increases their public interaction and interface  
- Green revolution and Linguistic organisation of states have given rise to the dominant caste forming their own regional parties (1st democratic upsurge)  
- Mandalisation of indian politics  
- Shortcomings of national politics to meet regional aspirations  
- Chairismatic personality of regional leaders like Nitish kumar, Mamta banerjee, Naveen patnaik  
- Absence of strong opposition at the centre  

#### Socio-economic
- Mobilisation of people on cultural ,ethnic and religious grounds  
- politicisation of caste  
- green revolution gave rise to dominant caste asserting their power in political sphere  

---

## Role of regional parties
- posed challenge to one party dominant system  
- made centre more responsive  
- made politics more competitve and popular participation  
- widened choice for voters  
- increased political consciousness of the people and also their interest in politics  
- assumed inportant role in coalition govt  

### Disadvantages
- more importance to regioinal interests  
- narrow approach to the resolution of national and international issues  
- encouraged regionalism that have become hurdles to national integration  
- responisble for unresolution of inter state water disputes,border disputes

## Decline of Left
- It is said that, there is no left, left in India.  
- Currently the CPI(M) has only 3 MPs in india.  
- Praful bidwai- book Phoenix movement - talks about decline of left.  
- Says it is surprising that even though there is widespread poverty in India, still weak left in India.

## Coalition government
- 1989-99: immature coalitions  
- 99 onwards mature coalitions that are stable  

- Bidyut chakraborty in his book- winning the mandate- compares west and india on coalitions  
  - Coalition by design vs by political calculation  
  - Positive vs negative coalition  
  - Minimum winning coalition vs oversized(due to lack of trust)  
  - Idological coalition vs rainbow coalition  

- M P Singh  
  - Concept of parliamentary axis and federal axis.  
  - Says in coalition govts even smallest faction can punch beyond weight.  
  - In coalition politics there is not one PM but many PM. Role of PM is reduced to that of a coalition manager.  

- State parties getting strong makes India from quasi federation to quasi confederacy. Says whenever one party dominant system —> parliamentary axis becomes strong. In multiparty systems “federal axis becomes strong.”  

- Reasons for formation of coalition govts- The sectional support base of parties, their inability to move beyond their core support base to attract new voters, has forced parties to form pre-poll alliances aimed at transferring the votes of their core supporters to each other for maximising electoral gains  

---

## Merits and demerits of coalition govt

### Merits
- accomodation of diverse interests  
- more representative in nature  
- leads to consensus based politics  
- coalition politics strengthens the federal fabric of the indian political system  

### Demerits
- Unstable and prone to instablity—> anti defection law misused to bring down coalition govts  
- Critics have called coalition partners as super PM or ultra PM  
- the steering committee of the coalition partners acts as super cabinet and undermines the role of cabinet  
- possibility of smaller constituents of the coalition govt playing the role of king maker. They demand more than their strength in the parliament.  
- increases blame game among coalition partners over situation of policy lapses  

- 2nd ARC- ethics of coalition government is seriously strained when the coalition partners change partnerships midstream. It recommends that Constitution should be amended to ensure that a common minimum programme is framed before the elections.

- The growing salience of regional parties in the national decision-making process in the present era shows the participatory strength of Indian federalism
