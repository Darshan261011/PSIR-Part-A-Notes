## Intro
- A social movement is an organized effort by a large group of people to achieve a particular goal, typically a social or political one.  
- These are extra Parliamentary mobilisation in a democracy.  
- Hannah arendt’s “communicative action in public sphere” serves as inspiration for these movements.  

---

## Key points on New social movements
- The new social movements offer an alternative social imaginary to both capitalism and socialism as they were both the systems to be retaining the elements of domination and unfairness. Ij the words of MN Roy  
  “capitalism and socialism have only been able to fulfil partial needs of man.”  
- Taking a cue from focoult and Laski, they argue that social power can no longer be seen a centrally located in the state or the economy but instead it is exercised as well as resisted at the societal level.  
- The new social movements are indicators of the pulse of the people that they are no longer ready to accept the developmental paradigms that keep them out and precluded their participation.  
- The marginalised can assert themselves through these movements collectively. For example tribals in Niyamgiri along with local NGOs against Vedanta  
- Judiciary also becomes a companion in these grassroots movements to assert the rights of civil society. For example LGBTQ movement have asserted that rights with the help of judiciary  
- Such movements has made democracy vibrant and help realise the goal of political equality. For example movement by sub altern classes like women and by dalits  
- These movements are against the elitist theory of democracy that views democracy through the narrow vision of elections.  
- these movements present inability of political parties to mobilise demands of citizens in an effective manner.

## Human rights
- From 1980s World over there has been growth of consciousness about human rights  
- In india, HR movements were particularly enhanced after in the wake of national emergency, LPG reforms and democratic decentralisation through 73rd and 74th AA.  
- In 1976, People’s union for civil liberties and democratic rights was formed heralding HR movements in India  
- In India activist judges like PN Bhagwati institutionalised PIL, which proved to be revolutionary step in providing access to justice to the marginalised sections  
- Supreme Court in Maneka Gandhi case gave wide interpretation to article 21  

### Human rights concerns in india
- Large number of under trials  
- Poor situation in prisons (114%) occupancy  
- HR violation under AFSPA  
- Death through hunger  
- Rape issues—>cite NCRB data

## RC Guha in unquiet woods
- R c guha- Described Indian environmentalism as environmentalism of the poor  
- Unlike the “ecology of effluence” in the west, in India environmentalism is defined by survival. It revolves around competing claims over resources such as forests, fish, water ,etc  
- RC guha and Madhav Gadgil have identified four broad strands within the environmental movements in india  

### Four strands
- a Crusading gandhians-  
- b Ecological Marxists-

## Significance of chipko movement

### Achievements of Bahuguna
- Played imp role in opposing Tehri dam  
- In bifurcation of UP to create UK  
- Against liquor mafia in the hills  
- Beej bachao andolan to protect agricultural biodiversity from being wiped out by chemicals and HYV seeds due to green revolution  

### Chipko movement
- The Chipko movement was a non-violent agitation in present day UK in 1973 that was aimed at protection and conservation of trees. Led by bahuguna, Chandi pradad bhatt and Gauri devi  
- original Chipko andolan dates back to the 18th century and was started by Rajasthan’s Bishnoi community...
