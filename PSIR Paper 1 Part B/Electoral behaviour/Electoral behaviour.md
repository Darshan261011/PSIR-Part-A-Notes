## Determinants of voting behaviour
- Caste-  
  - Rajni kothari- Indian politics is casteist and caste is politicised. When formulating their election strategies the political parties always take into account the factor of caste  

- religion- political parties indulge in communal propaganda and expose the religious sentiments of the voters. Despite India being a secular nation no political party ignores the influence of religion in electoral politics  

- Language- Organisation of states 1956 on language basis clearly reflects the significance of language factor in Indian politics. The rise of DMK in Tamil Nadu and TDP in Andhra Pradesh can be attributed to linguism  

- Region- regional parties appealed to the electorate on the ground of regional identities and regional sentiments. Biju janta dal and TMC are examples of regional parties  

- personality- give ex of nehru, indira, atal bihari vajpayee  

- Money- many voters seek money or liquor or goods in return for their goods  

- Wave- Paul Brass explains a wave election is one in which a clear tendency begins to develop among the electorate in a single direction and in favour of a national party or its leader  

- Performance of the ruling party  

- Party identification- people who identify themselves with a particular party will always vote for the party irrespective of its omissions and commissions  

- Ideology- Some people are committed to certain ideologies like communism , capitalism, democracy, secularism etc  

- Political events preceding an election like war, murder of a leader, corruption scandal  

- Economic conditions at the time of election like inflation, food shortage, unemployment
