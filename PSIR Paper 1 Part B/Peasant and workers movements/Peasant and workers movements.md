## Role of trade unions
- Trade unions in india have been a junior partner of political parties with the agenda setting power vesting with the latter.  
- Modern workers started emerging in India in 19th-century with introduction of railways, Post and telegraph  
- Lala lajpat rai and gandhi took keen interest in hardships faced by workers  
- Gandhi’s first hunger strike was the Ahmedabad mill strike.  
- Bombay mill hands association founded by NM Lokhande, Ahmedabad mill association founded by gandhi and AITUC founded by NM Joshi.  

---

## Worker movements in pre independence
- At the earliest stage they were sporadic and unorganised in nature and hence were mostly in effective  
- After their consolidation under trade unions by the efforts of leaders like SS Bengalee(for rights of workers and children), NM lokhande(bombay mill hands association)and sasipada Banerjee(started bharat shramjeevi), they started to assert themselves in a systematic manner.  
- Ahmedabad textile labour association,  
- Inspired by russian revolution, setting up of ILO  
- Trade Unions Act 1926 and the Trade Disputes Act, 1929 which also gave a fillip to the growth of trade unionism in India. The former Act provided for voluntary registration and conferred certain rights and privileges upon registered unions in return for obligations. The latter Act provided for the settlement of industrial disputes.  
- Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938. An important provision of the Act, inter alia, to accord compulsory recognition of unions by the employers gave a big fillip to the growth of trade unionism in India.  
- after WW1 , trade unions gained a momentum. Tilak and chaman lal founded AITUC.
