## Intro
- Coined by GJ Holyoke in 1851. It is a contested concept in political science . For west, it means complete separation between religion and state( neutrality). In indian context it means state and religion have porous boundaries with state intervention in religion as the need arises(equidistant model)  

---

## Context
- Secularism as a concept is a child of christianity because there was a rise of Protestant movements demanding rolling back of God and limiting God to personal sphere.  
- Scholars like mach, Hobbes and Locke contributed to the evolution of Western ideas of secularism.  
- In india we call it dharma-nirpekshata.

## Rajeev bhargava
- says despite debates, indian seclularism and its meaning remains “painfully elusive.”  

### Features of indian secularism
- He explains indian secularism as combination of substantive and procedural model which he calls as principled distanced model.  
- Western notion of strict “wall of separation” is rejected .  
- indian model is a politically negotiated model between majority and minority.  
- No strict separation, porous boundaries - State activity, political decision-making and policy choices are free of the interference of religion. But this does not mean that politics and state action will have nothing to do with the need of religious reforms.  
- It should not be helping or giving any advantage to any religion. It should be simply carrying out the injunctions of the Constitution which created it for precisely this purpose  
- Its neutrality should be ensured by a regime of equality, rights and dignity in the same measure for all  
- Bhargava says the idea of secularism has been persistently misused and abused. He calls it as “party-political secularism.”

## On UCC- DPSP Art 44

### Intro
- UCC calls for formulation of common civil law for all religious communities in matters such as marriage,divorce,inheritance,adoption,etc  

### Based on
- Religions neutrality  
- Universal citizenship  
- Nationalism- presence of separate personal laws for minorities prevents their integration into the mainstream society, strengthening communal identity and weakening national identity  

- Creates alternate sources of power- state and religious pressure groups but acc to Hobbes, only one sovereign on one single territory  

- UCC is not about commonality of civil laws but about intervention of state in personal laws. It is called as “uniform “ as it ensures alignment of personal laws as per constitutional values, appearing uniform/overlapping.  

- Flavia Agnes- UCC not a silver bullet. The condition of Hindu women have not improved even after “secularisation of Hindu laws.”Personal laws is under concurrent list as to allow diversity.  

- Law commission- UCC is neither feasible nor desirable. Secularism cannot contradict plurality prevalent in society. A unified nation does not necessarily have uniformity across all spheres. Instead codify all personal laws on the touchstone of fundamental rights.  

- Satya Prakash  

- legal editor the Tribune- discrimination cannot be justified in the name of diversity.  

- shah bano case 1985 and sarla mudgal case 1995 supreme court wants parliament to enact ucc.  

- Yogendra Yadav has recently argued of retaining separate family laws while removing their illiberal provisions.  

### Conclusion
- UCC alone would not solve the problem, total reconceptualisation of personal laws based on democratic principles is the way forward
