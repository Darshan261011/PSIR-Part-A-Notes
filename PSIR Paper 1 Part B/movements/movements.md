## Gandhian movements
- Use of satyagraha as a mass movement  
- Using gramsci’s war of position and war of manoeuvre  
- Acc to bipin chandra- gandhi was a mass psychologist . Knew when the masses were enthusiastic and when they were exhausted.  
- Bipin said INM was the best example of gramscian movement wherein gandhi used both methods to achieve his ends  
- He integrated the hitherto divided sections of the society  
  - Men and women  
  - Workers and capitalists  
  - Upper caste and lower caste  
  - Hindus ans muslims  
- used S-T-S strategy for bargaining with the British for the rights of citizens.

## NCM (1920-22)
- Truly Gandhian mov. at pan india level.  

### Reasons
- Rowlatt act curbed civil liberties  
- Jallianwala Bagh  
- Hunter commission report  
- Support to General Dyer by British public  
- Economic distress due to WW1  
- Khilafat mov.  

- Nagpur session, 1920: Congress officially adopted “extra constitutional method” mass struggle led by Gandhi.  
- Gandhi: NCM means right to refuse co-op. with ruler who misrules  

### Features
- surrender of titles and honours  
- boycott of govt schools, colleges, foreign clothes  
- No tax campaign  
- opening of national schools, courts, khadi, rejection of untouchability, maintain Hindu-muslim unity, observe strict non-violence  
- Gandhi instructed that congress should reach mohalla level  
- mov. ended in 1922 after chauri chaura incident  

### Achievements
- It initiated the new era of radical and non-constitutional methods.  
- It led to the emergence of Gandhian leadership and Gandhian methods of satyagraha, swaraj and non-cooperation  
- Due to the attachment of the khilafat question, the movement exhibited significant Hindu-Muslim unity.  
- The NCM was marked by huge participation from women and lower castes.  
- It also led to the greater participation from students, peasants in INM  
- It led to the spread of nationalist ideas to the rural areas  

### Failures
- The movement took a violent turn at Chauri Chaura, much against the Gandhian principles of ahimsa. This revealed the immaturity and non-understanding on the part of Indian masses , of the Gandhian methods.  
- Some western educated elite class of Indian Businessmen didn’t support the movement due to the boycott of foreign goods.  
- Moreover, Gandhi’s economic experiment with Charkha and Khadi found limited buyers amongst consumers as well as sellers as it was expensive as compared to the foreign machine made clothes.  
- The movement took Communal colours in Kerala (Moplah rebellion).  

- Gandhi‘s decision to call off NCM was criticised by MN Roy, Nehru and the extremist faction comprising Bhagat Singh , Chandrashekar Azaad.  

- Roy said Gandhi was fearful that masses would capture power and reject patronage of Gandhi and the leadership of Congress.

## CDM (1930-31)
- One of the most successful movements of gandhi  

### Causes
- Simon commmission  
- Economic Hardship due to great depression  
- Put forward 11 demands some of which were  
  - Abolish salt tax  
  - Total prohibition on liquor  
  - Release political prisoners  
  - Abolish CID  

- Mention sarojini naidu—> dharsana salt works, C rajagopalachari in TN, VO Chidambaram pillai in Andhra,etc

## QIM
- Most successful gandhian movement  
- It was a spontaneous and leaderless movement as major INC leaders were arrested a day before formal launch of the movement  
- Masses became their own leaders  
- Parallel govts in  
  - Ballia under chittu pandey  
  - Jatiya sarkar under Tamluk in Midnapore,Bengal  
- not supported by Hindu mahasabha, Muslim league, communists, princely states  
- No communal riots  
- Gandhi allowed use of force to defend oneself  

- He said “Do or die” but don’t live up to see the nation in slavery.  
- It was the final nail in the coffin  
- He said it was the last struggle of his life.  
- QIM was an example of classes in itself turning into class for itself. From war of position to war of manoeuvre.  

### Reactions
- Ambedkar called it a misadventure of gandhi  
- Roy- Gandhi is strengthening fascist forces  
- Francis hutchins  
  - Said QIM was the most ungandhian of all gandhian movements  
  - But says gandhi was a politician who knew there cannot be any mass struggle without violence(no birth without bloodshed)  
  - At this point swaraj became more important than non violence  

- Conclusion- Gandhi has been rightly called as the “mahatma” who led india to its destiny of independence.

## Revolutionary movements
- Used violence to strike terror in the hearts and minds of British  
- Believed in performing individual acts of heroism involving loot,assassination of officials, through the use of arms and ammunitions  
- Particularly aimed to inspire youth of the country to join the independence movement  

### Influence
- BC chatterjee- Anandmath  
- Sachin sanyal- bandijivan  
- Russian revolution and Irish revolution  
- Defeat of Russia by Japan,an asian power  

- played an important role during intermediate periods when Congress led activities were in passive mode  

- aurobindo ghosh and barindrakumar ghosh- Alipore bomb case members of anushilan saminit-Yugantar magazine. Also started the secret society: Anushilan samiti  
- Ghader party formed in San Francisco after the komagatta maru incident under leadership of Lala hardayal  
- London: shyamji krishna varme, VD savarkar at india House  
- Berlin: Virendranath Chattopadhyaya  
- Other parts of Europe: Madam Bikaji Cama and Ajit Singh  
- Kakori dacoity by members of HRA  
- Assasination of saunders and throwing of bomb in central legislative assembly  
- Delhi conspiracy case after the assassination attempt on Lord hardinge(then viceroy) by Rash behari bose  
- Chittagong armoury raid led by surya sen.  

### Their belief
- N0 personal sacrifice too great for the good of their country.  
- Denied “benevolent guidance” and control of the English.  

- Led to liberation of women like Bikaji cama, Pritilata waddedar , kalapana Dutta  

### Drawbacks
- Extreme patriotism and its methods of violence - the revolutionary movement could not become as broad as Gandhian .  
- Large sections of women and elderly could not afford to go for such extreme and violent methods .  
- The support for violence too was somewhere in conflict with cultural ethos of India.  
- The use of force gave an excuse as well as justification for British to unleash force on its part as well.  

- Bipin chandra- they removed the fear of authority and revive the lost pride of “manhood” amongst indians
