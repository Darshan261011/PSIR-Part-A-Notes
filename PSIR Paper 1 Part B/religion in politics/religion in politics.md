## Intro
- Political sociology approach of comparative politics studies interface of politics with societal factors like ethnicity,caste,religion,etc  
- Indian soceity is a prismtaic society and hence the role of identity factor like religion takes a dominant role.  

---

## Historical context
- Religion has been present in indian politics since pre independence time.  
- Policy of divide and rule by British, formation of Muslim league,separate electorates and partition of india on religious lines, all show how religion is entrenched in indian politics.  

---

## Post 1990s developments
- Role increased after 90s in india after the Ram janmbhoomi movement known as mandal and kamanadal politics.  
- Has led to communalisation of politics in india.  

---

## C Jafferelot

Acc to him caste politics has reached to an optimal level( since capping of reservation and no reservation still in pvt sector), religion remains the only option left for mobilisation of masses.

## Paul brass
- Analysed the communal violence in India( 3 stages of communalism)  

1. Preparatory stage- role of fire tenders  
2. Precipitatory stage- role of conversation experts( from a communal riot to blood bath)  
3. Explaintory stage- blame game starts  

- According to him everybody loves good riots in India. All parties are benefitted. Voters of all parties get consolidated and polarised  

---

## Causes behind communalism
1. Essentialists- Louis Dumont - people essentially communal and hence bound to conflict (strengthens two nation theory)  

2. Instrumentalist- bipin Chandra-people are not communal but elites in India mobilise people on the lines of religion. Hence religion becomes an instrument of elites
