### Ronald Dworkin's equality of resources that aims to achieve distributive justice
* Wrote the book **sovereign virtue** where he says equality is the sovereign virtue.

#### Equality of Resources: Imaginary Market Procedure
1. 100 shipwrecks who land on a deserted island
2. **Initial position: equal auction:** All 'equal' participate in fair bidding for resources of their choice; each one given 100 shells to buy resources
3. **Envy test:** by own choice and conception of good, each one buys bundles of goods; no one is envious of other's bundle of goods
4. **Intermediate phase:** individuals produce, invest, trade with different choice/preferences
5. **End result:** Inequality of outcome- resources, Income, wealth; outcome reflect personal choices and ambition
6. **Fair Insurance market:**
    * Out of 100 shells, each one buy suitable insurance as per one's risk taking aptitude
    * Risk pooling or luck sharing for brute bad luck - disability, sickness, less natural talent, bad option luck

* Acc to dworkin: just society is where initial distribution of resources is just. Hence he talks about resource egalitarianism. Hence his work's particular focus is on initial distribution.
* Dworkin sets out to establish a distribution that is **'ambition-sensitive'** but **'endowment-insensitive'**, so that differences in people's holdings are traced to what he calls their different 'ambitions', their differing goals, values and preferences, for example, but not to their 'endowments', to the talents they are born with or the resources with which they initially approach life.
* If people are provided with equal initial endowments, then if one person chooses to lead a more expensive life than another the resulting inequality is not unjust. 
* Whatever their personality, people should bear consequential responsibility for their choices. 
* It is only when people are worse off as a result of the unequal circumstances they find themselves in that they should not be held consequentially responsible, and have a case for compensation.
* Gave the concept of **brute luck** and **option luck** and state should compensate for brute luck(where a person does not has a choice) and not option luck(when a person had choice but he fucked up). Says person should not be held responsible for circumstances he has not chosen (brute luck)

### Criticism of dworkin's concept of equality

1. **Sen- gives capability approach.** * Says simple giving initial equal resources is not enough. If people are not capable enough to use their resources then it would eventually lead to inequality. 
    * For example, literacy is a capability, while reading is a function. 
    * In a society where people are illiterate, a state should actively promote people's ability to read, i.e. literacy. 
    * Capability advocate would argue that more than a provision of external means what matters is the capability-an internal ability-of the target population to read and write.

2. **Michael walzer-equality is a comlex phenomena.** * We dont even know the types of inequality that exist. 
    * gives **Concept of blocking the exchanges.** Need to block exchanges between different spheres so that inequality in one sphere does not affect attemts to create level playing field in another sphere.

3. **Communitarian scholars:** * Dworkin gives too much emphasis on choice(ambition). And not all ambitions are of our own will. They are also determined by our communities.

4. **Difficulty of distinction:** exact difference between "bad luck" and "bad choice" is difficult to make.
5. **Moral obligation:** should we not help one who is misfortunate to take bad decision?
6. **Implementation:** his theory is complex, idealistic and vague; difficult to implement.

### Comparison with Rawl's theory of justice
* **Thinner veil of ignorance:** people know a lot more about themselves in this case
* **Distinction of causes:** Makes distinction Between worst off because of brute luck or option luck(conscious choice) while rawls does not make any such distinction. For him all worst off are equal no matter the reason
* **Nature of theory:** Normative theory of equality rather than Rawls procedural theory of justice
* **Focus:** Rawls talks about equality of opportunity whereas Dworkin talks about equality of resources.

---

### Comparison with Nozick's theory of entitlement
* For nozick whoever finds a resource first, will own it rather than going for auction as dworkin says
* For dworkin, state takes care of misfortune or bad luck through a fair insurance market. For Nozick, no obligation on state to take care of the worst off.

### Other Perspectives on Equality
* **Iris Marion Young:** calls for differentiated equality and affirmative action while deciding the initial distribution.
* **Marxists:** call this liberal conception of equality as formal and procedural and call for absolute/substantive equality. Their main focus is equality of outcome rather than initial equality.
* **Alexis de Tocqueville:** the history of human society is the history of expanding the idea of equality

### Equality and liberty
* Equality is defined as "absence of privilege" while liberty is defined as "freedom to do what one wants to."
* These concepts first took shape during American and French Revolution.
* **Debate between socialists and capitalists:** The former puts equality above liberty while the latter puts liberty above equality.

#### Equality as Impediment to Liberty
* **F A Hayek:** * said inequality is bound to emerge as in even condition of equality people with greater skill will go ahead. 
    * State should not intervene to promote equality of outcome as it would destroy individual freedom. 
    * Says social justice is a mirage. Liberty is worth pursuing.
* Equality gives state a lot of power to curb liberty.
* Excessive focus on equality of outcomes prohibits liberty and disincentivizes people to work hard. This creates lower resources for distribution and further perpetuates inequality.

#### Equality and Liberty as Complementary
* **Rawls:**
    * reconciles the two principles in his theory of Justice. 
    * says inequality can only be justified when it maximises the position of least advantaged(difference principle)
    * Liberty as precondition for equality is explained by Rawls in lexical order. 
    * Inequality as an outcome of fair distribution of opportunity.
    * Difference principle- only after equal liberty and equality of opportunity there will be a need for helping those who are most disadvantaged.
* **Amartya sen:** Liberty holds no value unless people are made equal in terms of capabilities (capability approach).
* **Herbert A. Dean:** Liberty and equality aren't in conflict, they are just different facts of the same ideal.
* **Laski and MacPherson:** regard Both as complementary principles. Laski even said that those who say equality must not be obstacle in the way of liberty, they actually did not understand the true meaning of liberty itself.
* **Indian govt:** has reconciled equality and liberty through its AA program(reservation to SEBC).

#### Contemporary Context
* This debate was said to be ended after **Francis fukuyama's** end of history thesis with the victory of liberalism(liberty) over communism (equality).
* **Thomas pickettey's work - "capital in 21st century":** talks about growing inequality the world over and how the lack of equality is a threat to freedom itself (aristotle's theory of revolution).

### Conclusion
* For scholars like **Aristotle, Rawls**, equality should be proportionate. 
* For scholars like **Marx, Gramsci**, equality should be absolute. 
* Thus, it is quite clear that equality is essential for the existence of freedom.
