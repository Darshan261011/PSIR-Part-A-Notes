# Liberalism

### Introduction
* **Liberalism** is a political doctrine that takes protecting and enhancing individual freedom to be the central theme of politics.
* **Chronology:** Classical $\rightarrow$ modern $\rightarrow$ neo $\rightarrow$ social liberal

---

### Classical Liberalism
* **Key Figures:** Locke, Bentham, Mill
* **Core Characteristics:**
    * Ideology of the enlightenment era
    * All are equal since all have reason
    * Man is atomistic
* **John Locke:**
    * Concept of equality in political sphere, but not in socio-economic sphere.
    * Man is supreme.
    * Humanism as the central theme.
* **View of Society:**
    * Society is not natural but result of contract and must further man's interests.
    * Everybody's interests are different and sum of everyone's interest is aggregate interest. 
    * Conception of society is aggregative view of society (market model). 
    * Do not believe in collective view of society.
* **View of State:**
    * State is necessary (as man has passions) evil (restricts freedom).
    * "That state is best which governs the least" — **Thomas Jefferson**.
* **Equality:**
    * Talk about proportionate equality as opposed to socialists talking about absolute equality (substantive view of equality).
    * Liberal view of equality is formal and procedural.
* **Liberty:** Negative liberty.
* **Reform:** Due to Bentham, liberalism became a vulgar defence of utilitarianism. Mill tried to reform it by saying liberty and not utility is the core value of liberalism.
* **Social Policy:** * "Drunkard in the gutter is where he is supposed to be" — **Sumner**. 
    * Don't believe in welfarism as that would require taxation.
    * Don't believe in progressive taxation but progressive individualism.
* **In Service of the Republic (Vijay Kelkar):** "Government intervention interferes with personal freedom", as it is "imperfect" and has "unintended consequences".

---

### Modern Liberalism / Welfare State
* Bring idealism in liberalism to save it from crude economic determinism.
* **Mill:** not utility but liberty is the core theme of liberalism.
* **View of Man:**
    * Man is social by nature hence obligation towards society.
    * Man is diff from pig. Not completely utilitarian but also rational. 
    * Can think about himself and others (developmental perspective of man).
    * Reject atomistic concept of man as propounded by classical liberals.
* **View of Society:** Not a market mechanism but an organic entity.
* **View of State:**
    * Unlike idealists, dont say state is prior to man.
    * Unlike liberals dont believe state is a necessary evil. 
    * State is an instrument created by man for his happiness/pleasure. 
    * Like idealists they believe state is a source of virtue (**TH Green**), ensure welfare of citizens.
    * Advocate affirmative action to restore the dignity of the poor.
* **Core Principle:** Positive relation between man, society and state. 
* It is a golden mean of socialism and classical liberalism (**Herald J Laski**).
* **Immanuel Kant:** Concept of human dignity.
* **T H Green:**
    * Visualized welfare state.
    * Liberty is capacity to do something which is worth doing in accordance to the law of our being.
    * State hinders the hindrances that come in the path of liberty.
    * "Human consciousness postulates liberty. Liberty demands rights, rights demand state."

### Asian Values
* **Mahathir Mohammed:** Gave concept of Asian values where man is part of society.
* **Characteristics:**
    * Against universal declaration of human rights.
    * These are an attempt to impose western culture on Asians.
    * Give importance to community over an individual.
    * Rights are not absolute and they may be sacrificed for the security of the state.
* **Criticism:**
    * Communitarian scholars: man is a situated self and not atomistic.
    * The Left: Liberal society does not do enough to root out deep-seated racism, sexism, and other forms of discrimination.
* **Francis Fukuyama (Liberalism and its discontents):** Liberalism doesn't tell a person how to live. This produces a vacuum at the core of liberal societies, one that often gets filled by consumerism or pop culture... that do not necessarily lead to human flourishing.

### Libertarianism / Neoliberalism / Washington Consensus
* Call taxation as bonded labor and aggression on personality of man.
* Rolling back of state due to pressure of excessive welfarism.
* Revival of nightwatchman state.
* **Market Premise:** Markets lead to a desirable outcome (Pareto optimal outcome) so there is no need for government intervention.
* **Critique of Welfare:** Welfare state converted state into a nanny state that took care from cradle to grave.
* **Market vs. Government:** Market is mightier than the government. The government can control everything but not the market.
* **Advocates:**
    * **Margaret Thatcher:** Coined TINA — There is no alternative.
    * **Ronald Reagan**
    * **Milton Friedman (Capitalism and Freedom):** Capitalism is precondition for freedom.
    * **Robert Nozick (Anarchy, State, and Utopia):** Minimal state is inspiring and right.
    * **F A Hayek (Father of Neoliberalism):**
        * Critic of planning; calls it a fruitless exercise and road to serfdom.
        * Market is a huge central nervous system.
        * Calls social justice through welfarism as mirage.
    * **Murray Rothbard:** Calls politicians and bureaucrats as band of thugs.
* **Criticism:**
    * **Karl Polanyi:** Neolib subordinates the substance of society to the laws of the market.
    * **Arun Maira (A Billion Fireflies):** Liberal economics creates illiberal societies; inequality is leading to rise in authoritarianism and nationalism.

### Cornwall Consensus
* Aims to create economies that are:
    1. **Sustainable:** Focus on Green economy; use of resources as a common good.
    2. **Equitable:** Involvement of Government through PPP modules; focus on social sector with profit-sharing.
    3. **Resilient:** Balanced economy which identifies signs of problems beforehand.

### Social Liberals / Liberal Egalitarians
* Humane governance; emphasize on human dignity, capacity building and positive liberty (Post WW2 concept).
* Role of civil society to ensure accountability of both pvt and govt sector.
* Endorse market economy but state intervention in health and education is necessary.
* **Joseph Stiglitz (Globalization and its Discontents):**
    * Need to address democratic deficit in global institutions.
    * Concept of "asymmetric information": free markets yield bad outcomes for wider society when access to knowledge is unequal.
    * Level of inequality is "intolerable".
* **Amartya Sen:** Welfarism as constructive role of state; state must increase the capacity of masses.
* **Isaiah Berlin (Two Conceptions of Liberty):**
    * Champion/Soldier of liberty.
    * **Value Pluralism:** Values are inherently pluralistic.
    * **Negative Liberty:** Absence of interference (Hayek, Nozick, Mill).
    * **Positive Liberty:** Capacity to do what one wants (TH Green, Rousseau); liberates humans by developing them.
    * **Preference:** Prefers negative liberty; positive liberty gives rise to totalitarian state.
    * "A bird in a cage is unable to fly, there is lack of -ve liberty, a bird outside cage cannot fly is absence of capacity and not liberty."
    * Defining feature of liberalism is toleration.

---

### Freedom & Liberty
* **Freedom:** Considered as whole things; one can do whatever he desires (no law/rule).
* **Liberty:** A part of freedom; one can do what he desires but it should not affect other's liberty or damage public property.
* **Example:** India got freedom from British Government (can frame own laws), but Indians got liberty from their own country.

### Conclusion
* Liberalism has evolved with changing needs of time. 
* Helped realise importance of individual freedom, limited authority of state, and promoted ideals of humanism and rationalism.
