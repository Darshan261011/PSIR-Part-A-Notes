### End of history
* **Francis fukuyama:** Gave this concept At the end of cold war in 1989. 
    * **His words:** what we are witnessing is not just the end of Cold War but the end point of man's ideological evolution. The end of Cold War, collapse of communism shows that there is no better way of life than Western liberal democracy as the final form of human government.
    * Used **Hegel's dialectics** to show that lesser truth (communism) will eventually be eliminated causing end of dialectics.
    * Inspired from **alexander kojève** Who argued that history will end in the establishment of a universal homogeneous state And **george kennan** who gave the concept of spiritual vitality. The country that can give better standards of living to its people would ultimately win the cold war.
    * Concept of christian mythology that entire world come under Jesus.
* **Liberalism and its discontents (article by fukuyama):** Liberalism doesn't tell a person how to live. Thisproduces a vacuum at the core of liberal societies, one that often gets filled by consumerism or pop culture or other random activities that do not necessarily lead to human flourishing.

### Criticism of end of history / Clash of Civilisation (COC)
* **Benjamin R. Barber (Jihad vs. McWorld):** Talks about clash between corporate culture and religious orthodoxy.
* **seumas milne (revenge of history):** says authoritarian regimes in Saudi Arabia and Bahrain are supported by western governments. political traditions, he insists, have blood on their hands, and liberalism is certainly no exception.
* **Samuel p Huntington:** * The iron curtain of ideological conflict is being replaced by the velvet curtain of clash of civilisation.
    * Rather than "which side are you on?" he wrote, the overriding question in the post-Cold War world would be "who are you?"
    * Says- Western intervention in the affairs of other civilisations to promote democracy (called as neoconservatism in foreign policy that argues for using military means to promote democracy) is probably the single most dangerous source of instability and potential global conflict in a multi-civilisational world.
