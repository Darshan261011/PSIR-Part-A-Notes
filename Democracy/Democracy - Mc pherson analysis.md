# C.B. MacPherson: Analysis of Democracy

### Source Work
* **Democratic Theory: Essays in Retrieval**

---

### Core Conclusions
1.  **Monopoly over Democracy**
    * Liberalism can't claim Monopoly over democracy. 
    * Communitst model of one party state can also be called democracy if it has intra party democracy. 
    * They need not follow the western model of democracy. 
    * China calls itself as "democracy that works."

2.  **Types of Power**
    * **Extractive:** power in the form of coercion.
    * **Developmental:** power used for development.

3.  **State of Democracy in the Western World**
    * Mac is disappointed with the state of democracy in the western world.
    * **Inegalitarian Society (Western Countries):**
        * **Capitalists:** 99-100% Extractive | 99-100% Developmental.
        * **Workers:** 0-1% Extractive | 0-1% Developmental.
        * Capitalists have both types of power & workers have negligible power.
    * **Egalitarian Society:**
        * **Extractive:** 0%
        * **Developmental:** 100%
    * **Goal:** Western countries should make effort to achieve a situation when no one exercises an extractive power on the other hand each has equal developmental opportunities or opportunities for creative freedom.

---

### Models of Democracy

#### 1. Classical
* **Protective model (Locke, Bentham):** to protect rights of people.
* **Development model (Mill):** for overall development of an individual.

#### 2. Contemporary
* **Elitist (Schumpeter):** For them democracy is all about free and fair elections.
* **Pluralist (Robert Dahl, Charles Lindblom):** For them it is the pressure groups that play the main role (polyarchy and deformed polyarchies). Analysis is in line with pluralist theory of sovereignty (Laski).

---

### MacPherson’s Critique
* **Classical model:** lacks the empirical understanding of real world.
* **Contemporary model:** lacks the substantive aspect.
* **Schumpeter-Dahl Axis:** Coined term to describe both analyses as a procedural view of democracy.
* **Proposed Alternative:** Egalitarian model.
    * Should result into creative freedom.
    * Maximisation of developmental power.
    * Elimination of extractive power.

---

### Conclusion (Ludwig Wittgenstein)
* "While raisins may well be the better part of a cake, a bag of raisins is not a cake. Similarly while elections may be an integral part of a democracy, they cannot be its end."
