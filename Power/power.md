## Intro:
* Max Webber defined power as the ability to force one's own will upon the behaviour of others
* Power is the central concept of political science What money is to economics, power is to politics
* Steven lukes classified theories of power into 3 types:
    1. Power as decision making- Hobbes - in which state makes the decision
    2. Power as agenda setting- Marx - where captialists set the agenda
    3. Power as thought controlling process- hegemony/interpellation

## Michael foucault
* Takes micro view of power which is also known as sociological view of power/post modernist view. Inspired from Nietzsche.
* Power not just resides in state institutions(as said by hobbes) and also not with propertied class as said by marx, but flows through out the society like blood in the network of capllaries.
* Power is everywhere. No specific source and no specific direction, multidimensional.
* We are not only under power, we too exhert power.
* Wherever there is power, there is resistance, ex against the power of heterosexuals is the resistance of homosexual.
* Power is not just destructive but rather productive as it produces identity and stability in the society.
* He suggests that power is a product of discourses that is set of value or norms at a particular time. Thus he argues that knowledge is power (knowledge power connection)
* Gave concept of disciplinary power. In this people on whom it is exercised feel it is in their interest. They themselves govern themselves and hence it becomes convenient for the state to rule. It develops a "governed mentality." Society is watching us and discipline in society is build by discourses.
* If one wants to understand surveillance as a political concept, one cannot escape Frenchman Michel Foucault.
* For Foucault, knowledge is an inseparable twin of power, so much so that one doesn't exist without the other.
* If power were a multi-storey building, then knowledge is its very foundation. If any body or any institution wants to exercise power over you, then that entity first must have some basic knowledge about you. The deeper the knowledge, the more effective is the power that is applied.
* For instance, if the authorities want to apply their power to fine errant motorists on a busy street, they have to first have the knowledge of such drivers. That knowledge comes from CCTV cameras.
* The idea behind the exercise of sovereign power is to control people's "bodies" and "minds" so that their behaviour could be changed to what is considered the norm or "normal" and even lawful.
* Power helps make citizens comply with what is deemed correct. The repeated exercise of power aims to create self-correcting obedient citizens. In some cases, this is necessary. You will, for instance, automatically stop at traffic red lights.
* However, the exercise of power can be unequal, unjust and even work to benefit some, disadvantaging others.
* So, "where there is power, there is resistance..." Foucault writes.
