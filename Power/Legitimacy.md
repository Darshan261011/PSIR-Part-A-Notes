## Intro: 
* Legitimacy is the acceptance of an authority, usually a governing law or regime.

---

## Theory of legitimacy given by Max weber: 
* marx had said state will be overthrown. 
* But weber says state does not rule with naked power but with legitimate power. 
* It has consent Of the governed. 
* this consent comes out of the social contract(hobbes, locke, Rousseau).

### Rousseau
* says state will not be able to govern itself if people think its laws are not legitimate.
* Law represents legality but it can only be enforced when it reflects consent.
* In case consent is not reflected it gives rise to "crisis of governability."
* Indian state suffers from this in Kashmir and north east (Atul Kohli).
* In such cases, states have no option but to go down heavily. Althusser calls it coersive state apparatus.
* It may further challenge governability and result in collapse of state. 
* To avoid this, indian state under article 371 gives special powers to certain states which gives them rights to accept certain laws only if it suits their traditions and culture.

## Crisis of legitimacy
* given by Habermas (Neo marxist) from Frankfurt school(critical school of marxism) in his book - Legitimation crisis.
* He is critical of the welfarism adopted by capitalist democracies worldwide. 
* He says it has been done to prevent any revolt by the proletariats and says such model is unsustainable in the long term. 
* says welfare state is bound to suffer from legitimation crisis as it is based on two conflicting principles (captialism and socialism).
* In liberal democracies, political parties in order to gain power attempt to woo voters on lavish welfare schemes. 
* But the resources to fulfill such demands comes from a capitalist economic system. It is here that lies the contradiction
* He suggest that liberal democracies cannot permanently satisfy both the popular demands for social security and welfare rights, and the requirements of the market economy based upon private profit. 
* Hence the capitalist democracies will find it increasingly difficult to maintain legitimacy
* There is a limit to. Which capitalists would give money through taxation.
* in times of crisis, the govt would have to roll back and reduce fiscal deficit and welfare schemes which will cause resentment in masses.

### Habermas and Legitimation
* For Habermas, the instrument of creation of legitimacy is democratic system, maintenance of rule of law, freedom of press, etc 
* Legitimation crisis represents a decline in the confidence and acceptance of administrative functions, institutions or leadership. 
* It shows the institutions are failing to deliver the social goals for which they were instituted (violation of social contract)
* the demnads of population outstrip govt capacity to deliver. 
* Anthony king has called it "govt overload."
* A prime example of this is in the process of industrialization, where the establishment of factories and massive workforces often precedes the establishment of government regulations, workers' rights and labor unions. This then takes form of protests and social movements
* Arab spring in 2011 is a textbook example of legitimation crisis
* Greece and Venezuela are example of failure of unbridled welfarism.
