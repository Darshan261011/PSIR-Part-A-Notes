## Intro:
- In political science multiculturalism reflects a debate about how to understand and respond to the challenges associated with cultural diversity based on ethnic, national, and religious differences.

## Exponents:

### 1. Will kymlika
- book- multicultural citizenship : a liberal theory of minority rights.

- a. a liberal multiculturalist.
- b. Says liberalism is not about liberty but about tolerating others culture.

- c. In the age of globalization and growth of ethnic violence states must go beyond the idea of universal citizenship and go for multi cultural citizenship. universal citizenship ignores differences in cultures and leads to homogenisation.

- d. Says equal citizenship needed to build a community but not enough to deal with deep sense of alienation felt by these communities.

- e. Says ignoring multiculturalism leads to color blindness

- f. Argues for Special rights for minorities:
  - ◦ special cultural and educational rights
  - ◦ Special representation rights due to their numerical disadvantage
  - ◦ Self govt rights
  - ◦ Judicious affirmative actions
  - ◦ State support for minority institutions

- But for kymlika Rights are not universal. He limits special rights to national minorities and not immigrants as they have come by choice hence adopt to mainstream culture.

- Dworkin had said that a person must not be held responsible for circumstances he had not chosen, so a person must not face discrimination for the sake of his being born in a minority community.

- Give ex of ganga jamuna tahzeeb that shows intermixing of hindu and islam culture.

## Plural multiculturalist:

- if kymlika is liberal multiculturalist, Bhikhu parekh is plural multiculturalist.

### Bhikhu parekh
- book- rehtinking milticulturalism

- ◦ gives post colonial view of multiculturalism and criticises kymlika on taking liberal view of multiculturalism who believed that multiculturalism is an extension of liberalilsm.

- ◦ He asks for extending special rights to non citizen minorities also. He believes liberals have inherent sense of superiority.

- ◦ According to him principles governing human society cannot be based on the value system of a single culture.

- ◦ It has to be result of dialogue among civilisations.

- ◦ Praises India on its handling of a multi-cultural society.

- ◦ Gives harm principle- till the time it does not harm society and security of the state, minorities may be given some extra rights and be allowed certain cultural practice.

- ◦ Says We impose majority culture in the name of universalism. Sunday holiday for christians due to spread of colonialism. No harm if government gives holidays to Muslim workforce on Fridays

- ◦ Says multi-cul is a creative interplay of 3 important and complementary insights:
  - 1:every man is product of his culture
  - 2: every culture is inherently plural
  - 3: every culture has it's own conception of good life.

- ◦ Raimon Pannikar calls for a “diatopical” exchange – a dialogue between cultures – that societies can learn from one another, challenge each other and grow together, without being obliged to model themselves on one allegedly “higher” level of intellectual development.

## Value pluralism-

- In his book Four essays on liberty Isaiah berlin talks about value pluralism.

- ◦ It is the idea that there are several values which may be equally correct and fundamental, and yet in conflict with each other.

- ◦ In addition, value-pluralism postulates that in many cases, such incompatible values may be incommensurable, in the sense that there is no objective ordering of them in terms of importance.

- ◦ Value pluralism is opposed to value monism (that there is only one ultimate value).

- ◦ Thus, conflicts of values are intrinsic to human life. For this reason, Berlin encourages value pluralism. That is, efforts to understand others’ beliefs and commitments.

- ◦ Says the most defining feature of liberalism is not liberty but toleration.

- Exercise of liberty is based on a tolerant society.

- ( sidenote- bentham said utility is most imp value of liberalism, mill said liberty is most imp value of liberalism, berlin said toleration is the most imp value of liberalism)

- ◦ However, Berlin’s bias towards liberalism is evident in his belief that only within a society that respects liberty, can value pluralism be present.

- ◦ Berlin argued for the importance of liberty to choose from conflicting incommensurate values.

- ◦ Since globalisation has increased migration there is an increasing need of toleration.

- ◦ The clash of civilisations by Huntington has further supported the notion of clash of liberal values against oriental values
